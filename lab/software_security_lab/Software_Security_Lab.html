1. Introduction

The Software Security Laboratory of Suzhou Institute for Advanced Research, University of Science and Technology of China, was founded by Professor Xue Yinxing in 2018. Since its establishment, the laboratory has been committed to the research and development of AI system analysis, the direction of big data systems, blockchain smart contracts, software system security, etc., and has successively undertaken general projects of the National Natural Science Foundation of China and the Natural Science Foundation of Jiangsu Province. There are 2 items in total. At present, the main topics of the laboratory include: deep learning and software security, code similarity analysis, search-based software engineering, multi-objective operations research optimization, quantum computing, Ontology construction and formal verification based on protocol specification documents, and neural network similarity Research, blockchain smart contract security, automated understanding and formal verification of standard protocols, source code analysis and testing, safety testing of autonomous driving systems, etc. Research projects include: gray-box fuzzing system research based on difference comparison and attack surface-oriented, and software program analysis system for deep learning system.
The laboratory also undertakes the teaching work of the school's undergraduates. The courses offered include programming, frontiers of information security, theories and methods of software security, and introduction to software engineering.
The laboratory currently has 1 professor, 5 PhD students and 15 postgraduate students.
